Mental Disorders and Suicides around the World

Understanding the factors that contribute to suicides and mental disorders is fundamental in raising awareness in those that struggle with mental health conditions. According to the World Health Organisation, mental health awareness can increase understanding and reduce stigma surrounding mental health conditions, providing a gateway to increasing mental health care by first identifying the issues at hand.

Suicides and Mental Disorders around the World

The visualisation above captures the suicide rate per 100,000 population and highlights areas where suicide rates are most prevalent. Isolating by continent, Africa and Europe contain the largest proportion of suicide rates, followed by Asia, Oceania, North America and finally, South America. From 1997 to 2019, Africa overtook Europe in having the largest proportion of suicide rates.

Comparing suicide rates and mental disorders in the visualisation on the left, higher suicide rates appear to correlate with a higher prevalence in depressive disorders, as African countries have the highest prevalence of depressive disorders, which corresponds with their high proportion of suicides. Throughout 1990 to 2019, Greenland, Losotho and Guyana appear to be the countries with the highest suicide rates, which can also be attributed to their high prevalence in depressive disorders.

Suicides and Mental Disorder Demographics

The visualisation above compares the prevalence of mental disorders by female and male sex across different countries. Overall, females tend to have higher prevalence of mental disorders compared to males. In comparing the prevalence of eating disorders in males and females, it can be seen that females have almost two times the prevalence in eating disorders than in males.

Despite females generally having a higher prevalence in mental disorders, males have a significantly higher number of suicides across all age groups and continents and shown in the population pyramid above.

The visualation above captures the relationship between age groups and the prevalence of mental disorders. The highest prevalence of any mental disorder occurs in the age group 40-59 years, followed by 20-39 years. Across all continents and age groups, depressive disorders have the highest prevalence, followed by anxiety disorder.

The visualisation above captures the suicide rates across different age groups in the countries identified with the highest suicide rates: Greenland, Losotho and Guyana. Across the three countries, the age groups 40-59 years and 20-39 years have the highest number of suicides, which is consistent with the previous visualisation which showed that those age groups also contianed the highest prevalence in mental disorders.

Influences of Substance Disorders on Mental Health

The visualisation on the left captures the relationship between the prevalence of mental disorders and substance disorders. Higher prevelance of substance disorders (both alcohol and drugs) tends to correlate with a higher prevelance of depressive disorders. With anxiety disorders, there tends to be a greater prevalence of drug use disorders and the prevalence of anxiety disorders increases.
